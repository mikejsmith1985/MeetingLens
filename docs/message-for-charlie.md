I've set up a tool called **MeetingLens** to help you capture what's shown on screen during meetings and turn it into notes you can read. It's just a web page — nothing to install, and it works entirely by keyboard, so your screen reader will guide you the whole way.

Here's what to expect when you open it:

1. It opens ready to go, and lands you right on the **Start capture** button — just press **Enter**.
2. Your browser will ask what to share. Press **Tab** until you hear **Entire Screen**, select it, then **Tab** to **Share** and press **Enter**.
3. Go to your meeting as normal. MeetingLens quietly takes a screenshot each time the slide changes — you don't have to do anything.
4. When the meeting ends, come back to the MeetingLens tab. The **Stop** button will be selected — press **Enter**.
5. It saves everything as one file to your Downloads and copies a ready-made prompt for you. An **Open Copilot** button is then selected — press **Enter**, paste the prompt with **Ctrl+V**, and attach that one file. Copilot will describe each slide in order.

A few things to know:

- Your screen reader does all the talking — I've made sure the app won't speak over it.
- You really only do three things: Start, pick "Entire Screen," and Stop. Everything in between is automatic.
- Tip: press **Ctrl+D** to bookmark it so it's easy to find next time.

Give it a try before a real meeting if you'd like, and tell me how it goes — happy to walk through it together anytime.
