I've set up a tool called **MeetingLens** to help you capture what's shown on screen during meetings and turn it into notes you can read. It's just a web page — nothing to install — and I've set it up to work smoothly with **JAWS**. It's all keyboard, and JAWS will guide you the whole way.

Here's what to expect when you open it:

1. It opens ready to go, and JAWS announces the **Start capture** button — just press **Enter**.
2. Your browser then opens its own "share" box, which JAWS reads. Press **Tab** until you hear **Entire Screen**, select it, then **Tab** to **Share** and press **Enter**.
3. Go to your meeting as normal. MeetingLens quietly takes a screenshot each time the slide changes — you don't have to do anything, and JAWS announces each one.
4. When the meeting ends, come back to the MeetingLens tab. JAWS will have the **Stop** button selected — press **Enter**.
5. It saves everything as one file to your Downloads and copies a ready-made prompt. JAWS will then have the **Open Copilot** button selected — press **Enter**, paste the prompt with **Ctrl+V**, and attach that one file. Copilot will describe each slide in order.

A few things to know:

- JAWS does all the talking — the app won't speak over it.
- You don't need to switch JAWS modes; just press **Enter** on whatever button JAWS announces.
- You really only do three things: Start, choose "Entire Screen," and Stop. Everything else is automatic.
- Tip: press **Ctrl+D** to bookmark it so it's easy to find next time.

Give it a try before a real meeting if you'd like, and tell me how it goes — happy to walk through it together anytime.
